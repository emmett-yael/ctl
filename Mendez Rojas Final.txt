This flowchart is intended to guide those helping asylum seekers determine if the asylum seeker is a member of the Mendez Rojas class, which would give them a legal recourse for missing the one-year deadline for application by asylum seekers. </p>

Q(11): You are a member of Class B if:
o You were detained by DHS either (i) when you came to the United States; or (ii)
within fourteen days of entering the United States without permission;
o You told an immigration officer you were afraid to return to your country of origin
and then DHS released you from detention and gave you a Notice to Appear (NTA)
2
before an immigration judge in removal proceedings (Form I-862);
o The government did not notify you that your asylum application had to be filed within
one year of your arrival to the United States; and
o You are applying, or you applied, for asylum more than one year after the last time
you arrived in the United States.
• Subclasses:
o You are a member of Subclass B.I if you are not in removal proceedings.
o You are a member of Subclass B.II if you are in removal proceedings
Q(12): Class A membership
A: You are a member of Class A if:
o You were detained by DHS either (i) when you came to the United States; or (ii)
within fourteen days of entering the United States without permission;
o You received a screening interview with an asylum officer (a credible fear interview)
and DHS released you after the interview because the asylum officer found that you
had a credible fear of persecution or torture;
o The government did not notify you that your asylum application had to be filed within
one year of your arrival to the United States; and
o You are applying, or you applied, for asylum more than one year after the last time
you arrived in the United States.
• Subclasses:
o You are a member of Subclass A.I if you are not in removal proceedings.
o You are a member of Subclass A.II if you are in removal proceedings.
